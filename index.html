Financial Literacy

The Main Problem

  • According to a survey on Global Financial Literacy in 2012 conducted by VISA, only 35% of Indians were financially literate and India was among the least financially literate countries.
  • Kiliyanni and Sivaraman (2016) measured financial literacy among educated young adults in the Indian state of Kerala and found that they were able to answer only 44% of the questions on the survey correctly.
  • These statistics highlight the need for young Indian adults to improve their financial literacy, as studies link it to financial safety. With today's access to technology and information, students should use online tools to learn budgeting, saving, and investing to ease their transition to adulthood and ensure future financial stability.

Stock Market Information

Access to stock market information allows students to stay informed about market trends and investment opportunities. This knowledge is crucial for those interested in investing, helping them make better decisions about buying or selling stocks.

Budgeting Tool

A budgeting tool helps students manage their finances by tracking income, expenses, and savings. It provides a clear overview of where their money is going, helping them make informed decisions, avoid overspending, and set financial goals.

AI Budget Buddy

An AI Budget Buddy can provide personalized financial advice, making it easier for students to manage their money effectively. It can offer tips on budgeting, saving, and investing based on individual spending patterns and goals. With its knowledge of investing, it can help students learn how to grow their money over time.
